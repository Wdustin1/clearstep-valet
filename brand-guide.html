01 — Introduction

Brand Overview

These guidelines define how Valet Property Solutions presents itself across every touchpoint — from business cards and uniforms to digital platforms and client-facing documents. Consistency builds trust, and trust wins contracts. Every team member and vendor should follow these standards.

Professional
We serve commercial clients. Every interaction should reflect the seriousness of a B2B partner, not a side gig.
Reliable
We show up every night. Our brand should communicate the same dependability — clean, consistent, no shortcuts.
Local
We're not a faceless national chain. We're local operators who pick up the phone and solve problems same-day.
02 — Logo

Primary Logo

The VPS logo combines a V and P monogram with a roofline and checkmark, symbolizing property service and task completion. It is the cornerstone of our visual identity.

Full Color — White Background
White — Dark Background
Grayscale — Print Fallback

Clear Space

Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the "V" stroke on all sides. No text, graphics, or other logos should enter this zone.

V height V height V height V height

Minimum Size

Print: 1 inch wide minimum  |  Digital: 80px wide minimum  |  Below these sizes, use the icon mark (VP monogram only, no wordmark).

✓ Do

  • Use the logo on white, black, or dark gray backgrounds
  • Use the white version on any dark background
  • Use the grayscale version for single-color print
  • Keep the logo proportions locked (no stretching)
  • Center the logo when used as a standalone element

✗ Don't

  • Place on busy photos or patterned backgrounds
  • Rotate, skew, or distort the logo
  • Change the green to any other color
  • Add drop shadows, glows, or outlines
  • Rearrange the monogram and wordmark
  • Place on mid-tone backgrounds (use white or dark only)

✓ Color Usage

  • Green is for accents, CTAs, and highlights — never large background fills
  • Black or Charcoal for all major text
  • White or Off White for page/card backgrounds
  • Use the green gradient (Bright → VPS → Deep) sparingly for premium feel
  • Green on black = high contrast, great for digital and uniforms

✗ Color Misuse

  • Don't use green as a background color for text blocks
  • Don't use any blues, reds, or off-brand colors
  • Don't use light green text on white (fails accessibility)
  • Don't substitute similar greens — use exact hex values
  • Don't use green and charcoal together with no contrast (too dark)

✓ Type Rules

  • Use Plus Jakarta Sans for all headlines and display text
  • Use Inter for body copy, labels, and UI elements
  • System fonts (Arial, Helvetica) only as last-resort fallbacks
  • Headlines: tight letter-spacing (-0.02em)

✗ Type Rules

  • Never use decorative, script, or novelty fonts
  • Never use all-caps for body text (headings and labels only)
  • Don't use thin/light weights — minimum 400
  • Don't mix more than these two font families
05 — Voice & Tone

How We Communicate

Every piece of copy — emails, proposals, social posts, even text messages — should sound like it comes from the same company. We are professional but not corporate, confident but not arrogant.

✓ We Sound Like

  • "We'll have bins delivered and service running within 7 business days."
  • "Your nightly report is ready — 100% completion, zero issues."
  • "Let us know if anything needs attention. We're a call away."
  • "Here's how VPS adds $36,000/year to your property's NOI."

✗ We Don't Sound Like

  • "Hey! We'd LOVE to help with your trash!! 🎉🗑️"
  • "We are the premier synergistic waste solution provider."
  • "Hmu if you need trash picked up lol"
  • "As the industry-leading valet trash innovator..."

07 — Digital Standards
