# ClearStep Valet — Business Plan
**All In One Reality LLC, DBA ClearStep Valet**
Owner: Shannon Honse | Wichita, KS | Prepared: March 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

ClearStep Valet is a doorstep valet trash collection service serving apartment communities in Wichita, Kansas and surrounding areas. The company operates under the existing LLC, All In One Reality, doing business as ClearStep Valet.

The service model is simple: property managers pay a monthly per-unit fee. Valets collect trash bags from outside each resident's door, 5–7 nights per week, and haul them to the on-site dumpster or compactor. Residents never leave their floor. Property managers gain a premium amenity, reduced dumpster overflows, and additional net operating income.

**Service launch target:** Q2–Q3 2026
**First-year revenue target:** $50,000–$75,000
**Operating model:** Founder-operated, owner-managed routes, scaling with contract volume

## 2. The Opportunity

### Wichita Multifamily Market

- Wichita metro area population: ~650,000
- Estimated apartment units (Wichita metro): 30,000–40,000+
- Active new construction pipeline: ~800 new units added in 2024 alone (Wichita Business Journal)
- Average occupancy: 92.1% (Q1 2024)
- Average rents trending upward, market remains affordable vs. national average

### Why Now

- Valet trash is the #1 rated resident amenity in multifamily housing nationally
- No established local valet trash operator dominates the Wichita market
- National players (Valet Living, Trash Butler) focus on large metros — the 50–300 unit Wichita market is underserved
- Property managers under increasing pressure to reduce turnover; amenities are the lever

### Competitive Gap

Wichita property managers currently have two options:
1. Rely on residents to self-manage trash (leading to overflows, violations, complaints)
2. Contract with a national provider not focused on local service quality

ClearStep is the local alternative: owner-operated, accountable, responsive, and priced fairly.

## 3. Service Model

### Core Service: Doorstep Trash Pickup
- **Frequency:** 5–7 nights per week, per property schedule
- **Window:** Valets collect bags set outside doors between 7–10 PM, haul to dumpster by midnight
- **Equipment provided:** One 13-gallon bin per unit (included in service)
- **Team:** Shannon Honse (owner/operator), with additional help as route volume requires

### Pricing
- **Starting rate:** $10/unit/month billed to property management
- **Typical property manager pass-through to residents:** $20–35/month (added to rent)
- **Net spread to property manager:** $10–25/unit/month — service pays for itself and generates NOI

### Sample Economics (100-unit property)
| Line Item | Monthly |
|---|---|
| ClearStep charge to property | $1,000 |
| Property manager charges residents ($25/unit) | $2,500 |
| Property manager net gain | **+$1,500/mo** |
| Annual NOI boost | **$18,000** |
| Est. property value increase (6% cap rate) | **+$300,000** |

## 6. Operations Plan

### Nightly Route Structure
- Target: 75–120 units per 2-hour shift (solo)
- Walk time: ~90 seconds per unit in a dense building
- Equipment needed per route: cart, gloves, PPE, flashlight
- Route completion: midnight cutoff, all bags at dumpster

### Scheduling
- Property contracts specify 5 or 7 nights/week
- Resident window: bags out 7–10 PM
- Valet window: 8 PM–midnight
- Non-collection nights: bins stay at doors, no service

### Scaling
- Solo: 1 operator can handle 200–300 units across 2–3 properties per night
- First hire trigger: 300+ units (route overflow point)
- Hire model: part-time W-2 or 1099, depending on volume
- Note: Dustin (co-founder/helper) is available for overflow route support

### Quality Control
- Spot-check own routes monthly
- Property manager contact available 24/7
- Response SLA: same-business-day for complaints

## 7. Sales Strategy — 90-Day Client Acquisition Plan

**The biggest challenge: getting the first 3 clients.**

### Target Profile
- Apartment complexes: 75–300 units
- Wichita metro (Wichita, Derby, Andover, Maize, Haysville)
- Class B/C properties — Class A already expect premium amenities, but Class B is price-sensitive and the NOI pitch lands hard
- Property manager is the decision-maker (not the owner/LLC)

### Channel 1: Direct Outreach (Highest Priority)
1. Pull list of all apartment complexes 75+ units in Wichita (Apartments.com, Google Maps, CoStar free tier)
2. Identify property manager name/contact at each (call the leasing office)
3. Visit or call with pitch sheet in hand
4. Lead with the NOI math, not the service features
5. **Goal: 20 conversations in the first 30 days**

### Channel 2: Property Management Companies
- Several PM companies manage multiple properties
- One signed PM company can mean 3–5 properties immediately
- Targets: Block & Company, Occidental Management, local boutique PM firms

### Channel 3: Real Estate Investor Network
- Shannon's existing network through real estate activity
- Investors who own apartment buildings are the decision-makers
- Warm introductions bypass the PM gatekeeper entirely

### Channel 4: Digital (Support Channel)
- Website live: clearstep-valet.vercel.app
- Google Business Profile: list as "Valet Trash Service, Wichita KS"
- Facebook/LinkedIn: post in Wichita property manager groups

### Objection Handling

## 8. Legal & Compliance

- **Entity:** All In One Reality LLC (existing), operating as ClearStep Valet
- **DBA filing:** Register "ClearStep Valet" as trade name with Kansas SOS (recommended — ~$30)
- **EIN:** Needed for business banking/taxes — free at IRS.gov
- **General liability insurance:** Required before any service begins. Get COI naming property as additional insured for each contract.
- **Service agreements:** Each property should sign a service agreement covering: scope, per-unit rate, billing cycle, pickup window, cancellation terms (30-day notice recommended)
- **Background checks:** Consider running your own and carrying documentation — property managers will ask
